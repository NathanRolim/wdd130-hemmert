What it's for

Stat Vault organizes the information a Game Master needs during a Dungeons & Dragons session (and similar tabletop games) into one tool. Instead of juggling five browser tabs, three bookmarks, and a character sheet, you keep your stat blocks, dice presets, and encounter tables saved and ready to pull up at the table.

A few examples

Saved stat blocks

Store a monster's AC, HP, attacks, and abilities once, then load it the moment it enters the fight.

Dice presets

Save the rolls you reuse (random loot, wild magic, weather) and roll them against a table in one click.

Encounter tables

Group several stat blocks into a single encounter so the whole fight is ready before the party walks in.

Quick start

  1. Create a preset. Add a stat block or a dice table and give it a name you'll recognize mid-session.
  2. Save it to your catalog. Everything you save lives on the Presets & Dice page, ready to search.
  3. Build an encounter. Pull saved blocks together into an encounter table on the Builder page.
  4. Run your session. Open Stat Vault at the table and load what you need, when you need it.
